Jhilmilaa: Vibrance of Our Nation

Indian American Student Association, 2016

Role

Show Coordinator

Team

IASA Board

Status

Presented November 2016

Fueling the creative efforts for a student-led production

The Indian American Student Association (IASA) is a cultural student organization at the University of Michigan. The goal of the organization is to create a community by seeking to preserve and cherish our Indian culture while adapting to the ever-changing modern environment. One of the largest events that IASA puts together is the annual cultural show, where the organization and it’s members showcase dances of various Indian styles. This is one of the largest student-led performances in the country, with over 200 performers and 9 dance performances.

My role was as the coordinator of this event. With my co-chair and the team that we interviewed and selected, one of our responsibilities was to craft the theme, name, and logo of this year’s show.

One of the 9 dances that performed that night

Theme

The theme is the backbone of the show. It is the message we are trying to communicate through the show. Similar to a mission statement, all subsequent decisions are based off how well they tie back to the theme.

In order to come up with the theme we had all team members come up with a rough draft of an idea of a theme and how it could relate to the dances that would be featured and a logo. Since it was the summer, we had our team members present their ideas on a remote team meeting. After a couple sessions we are able to iterate and hone in on a single idea.

The initial document that we built the show off of

Name

The next thing we had to decide was the name for the show. We again prompted the group for suggestions. We developed some requirements in order to help everyone focus their thinking.

  • Indian origin - The name has to have an Indian origin (usually Sanskrit, Urdu, or Dravidian roots).
  • Pronounceable - The name has to be easy to pronounce by all people (not just people familiar with Indian languages).
  • Chant-able The name has to be able to be chanted. We know from past shows that the show name will be chanted by the audience during the show. We find that words with 3 syllables are the best for chants.
  • Relate to the theme - We needed a name that would relate back to the general theme.

We brainstormed names until we thought of and aligned on ‘Jhilmilaa’ (means shimmering in Urdu). We chose this as it met our requirements and enhanced our theme that was all about color and vibrance.

Logo

Finally, the last major item to figure out was the logo—the visual representation of our theme. We brought in an additional graphic designer to provide an alternative option, as I was the only one on the team with the graphic design skills to build out the logo. After finding some inspiring examples of colorful logos, we both set out to create logos based on our interpretation of the theme. I created my designs by drawing on a notebook and then recreating the design using Adobe Illustrator.

We ended up going with my designs, as the team found it more memorable and understandable.

My logo design in black and white. My designs were a reference to Holi, the Festival of Color. The colors were shaped as lotus petals—the national flower of India.
